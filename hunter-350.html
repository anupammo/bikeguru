Enfield 350

The Hunter 350 is a new cruiser bike from Royal Enfield that offers a blend of style, performance, and fun. It is based on the same platform as the Meteor 350 and the Classic 350, but has a distinct neo-retro roadster design that sets it apart from the rest. In this article, we will provide a detailed description of the Hunter 350, covering its features, specifications, accessories, competitors, and customer feedback. We will also give some tips and suggestions on how to make the most of your Hunter 350 experience.

Features

The Hunter 350 comes with a host of features that make it an attractive choice for bike enthusiasts. Some of the key features are: - The Hunter 350 is powered by a 349.34 cc, air/oil-cooled, single-cylinder engine that produces 20.2 bhp of power and 27 Nm of torque. The engine is fuel-injected and counterbalanced, which means it delivers a smooth and refined ride with minimal vibrations. - The Hunter 350 has a lightweight and agile chassis that enhances its handling and maneuverability. The bike has a shorter wheelbase, lighter weight, and tighter geometry than the other J-platform models, which makes it more responsive and fun to ride. The bike also has a low seat height of 800 mm, which makes it accessible and comfortable for riders of different heights. - The Hunter 350 comes with a telescopic fork at the front and twin shock absorbers at the rear for suspension. The suspension setup is well-tuned to absorb bumps and potholes on the road, while also providing stability and control at high speeds. The bike also has disc brakes on both wheels, with dual-channel ABS as standard, for effective and safe braking. - The Hunter 350 has a retro-modern design that combines classic elements with contemporary touches. The bike has a round headlamp, tail lamp, turn signals, and mirrors, along with a teardrop-shaped fuel tank and a single-piece seat. The bike also has a digital-analog instrument cluster that displays speed, odometer, trip meter, fuel level, gear position, battery voltage, and service reminder. The bike also comes with a USB charging port and Bluetooth connectivity for smartphone pairing. - The Hunter 350 is available in three variants: Retro Factory, Metro Dapper, and Metro Rebel. The Retro Factory variant has black and silver paint schemes with spoke wheels and single-channel ABS. The Metro Dapper variant has grey, ash, and white paint schemes with alloy wheels and dual-channel ABS. The Metro Rebel variant has red, black, and blue paint schemes with alloy wheels and dual-channel ABS. The Metro Rebel variant also has the most striking and snazzy graphics on the tank and side panels.
